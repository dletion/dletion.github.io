GIAS GIVES is a web-focused project centered around increasing alumni involvement, inspire donations, and make it easier for users to choose a cause to support within the College of Imaging Arts and Sciences at RIT.

This was an individual school project that involved extensive user research, wireframing, and visual design.

20 cups of coffee

October 2015

Photoshop, Illustrator

Features

Using visually stimulating images and clean visuals, visitors will find the site more appealing and inviting than before.

By simplifying the donation process, alumni will find it easier to complete transactions and more inclined to donate in the future.

Allowing users to choose different "causes" and majors for donations will increase engagement and interest in the projects and efforts of the students and staff within the College of Imaging Arts and Sciences.

Sketches

One of the major features involved in the redesign of CIAS GIVES was the ability to choose which 'causes' to donate to, instilling a sense of security in the donators. Another major aspect learned from user research was a need for clear information design about the funds aquired, the various majors, and the goals of the college as a whole. And to top this off, a simplified process for donations, taking up less of the user's time and encouraging future donations.

Final Screens

The goals of CIAS GIVES were to revitalize the current site's visuals and user experience. The visuals of GIAS GIVES are based upon research from alumni, and also from research on different successful charity websites. High quality visuals, clean organization, and good information design were key.
